Cyber Supply Chain Risk Management

The process of identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks related to the supply chain in the context of cybersecurity.

Description

Cyber Supply Chain Risk Management (C-SCRM) refers to the strategies and practices used to manage risks associated with the supply chain in the realm of cybersecurity. In today's interconnected world, organizations rely heavily on third-party vendors, suppliers, and partners for various products and services, including software, hardware, and IT services. This reliance creates potential vulnerabilities, as any weaknesses within the supply chain can be exploited by cyber adversaries. C-SCRM involves evaluating the security posture of suppliers, monitoring their compliance with security standards, and ensuring that they implement robust cybersecurity measures. Key components of C-SCRM include conducting risk assessments, establishing security requirements for procurement, continuous monitoring of supplier risk, and developing incident response plans that involve third parties. Effective C-SCRM helps organizations safeguard their data and systems from supply chain attacks, which can have far-reaching consequences, including data breaches, financial loss, and reputational damage.

Examples

  • A company conducts security assessments of all its software vendors to ensure they follow best practices.
  • An organization implements a multi-factor authentication process for access to critical systems by third-party contractors.

Additional Information

  • C-SCRM is essential for compliance with regulations such as NIST SP 800-161 and others.
  • Supply chain attacks, such as the SolarWinds incident, highlight the importance of C-SCRM.

Autonomous System (AS)

A collection of IP networks and routers under the control of a single organization that presents a common routing policy to the internet.

Description

An Autonomous System (AS) is a distinct entity in the context of internet routing, defined by the Internet Assigned Numbers Authority (IANA). It is a group of IP networks and routers managed by a single organization that operates under a unified routing policy. Each AS is identified by a unique Autonomous System Number (ASN), which is used in the Border Gateway Protocol (BGP) to facilitate inter-domain routing. Autonomous Systems can be classified as either 'stub' (connected to only one other AS) or 'transit' (capable of routing traffic between multiple ASes). The primary purpose of an AS is to enable efficient routing on the internet, allowing organizations to manage their own IP address space and control how their networks communicate with other networks. In terms of network security, understanding the structure and behavior of ASes is crucial, as it helps in identifying potential threats and vulnerabilities in the routing infrastructure. Misconfigurations or malicious activities at the AS level can lead to significant disruptions in internet connectivity or data breaches.

Examples

  • Example 1: An internet service provider (ISP) operating a large network that connects various customers and manages their internet traffic.
  • Example 2: A university network that connects its campus networks and research facilities, presenting a unified routing policy to the internet.

Additional Information

  • Additional info 1: ASes can be either public (advertising their routes to the internet) or private (not directly reachable from the internet).
  • Additional info 2: The concept of AS is fundamental to BGP, the protocol used for routing data between different ASes on the internet.

phishing

A cyber attack that uses disguised email or messages to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information.

Description

Phishing is a type of cyber attack that typically involves the use of fraudulent communications, often in the form of emails or messages, that appear to come from reputable sources. The main objective of phishing attacks is to deceive individuals into providing sensitive information, such as usernames, passwords, credit card details, or other personal information. Attackers often create a sense of urgency or fear to compel the victim to act quickly without thoroughly evaluating the legitimacy of the communication. Phishing can take various forms, including spear phishing (targeted attacks on specific individuals), whale phishing (targeting high-profile individuals), and vishing (voice phishing via phone calls). The consequences of falling victim to a phishing attack can be severe, leading to identity theft, financial loss, and compromise of sensitive organizational data. To protect against phishing, individuals and organizations are encouraged to employ security measures such as email filtering, user education, and multi-factor authentication.

Examples

  • An email that appears to be from a bank asking the user to verify their account information by clicking a link.
  • A message on social media that claims the user has won a prize and prompts them to provide personal information to claim it.

Additional Information

  • Phishing attacks can be highly sophisticated, using social engineering techniques to appear legitimate.
  • Awareness and training are key elements in preventing successful phishing attacks.

Attack Surface Management (ASM)

The practice of identifying, monitoring, and reducing the attack surface of an organization's digital environment.

Description

Attack Surface Management (ASM) is a critical aspect of network security that focuses on the continuous identification and assessment of an organization’s potential vulnerabilities and entry points that could be exploited by malicious actors. The 'attack surface' refers to the sum of all possible points where an unauthorized user could gain access to a system or network. ASM involves a systematic approach to discovering, analyzing, and managing these vulnerabilities across the entire digital landscape, including on-premises, cloud, and mobile environments. Organizations utilize various tools and techniques such as automated scanning, manual testing, and threat intelligence to regularly evaluate their attack surface. By maintaining an up-to-date inventory of assets and their associated risks, organizations can prioritize security efforts, remediate vulnerabilities, and reduce the likelihood of successful cyberattacks. ASM also encompasses the continuous monitoring of changes in the environment, ensuring that any new vulnerabilities are quickly identified and addressed, thereby enhancing the overall security posture of the organization.

Examples

  • Using automated tools to scan for exposed services and applications on the network.
  • Conducting regular penetration testing to identify vulnerabilities in web applications.

Additional Information

  • ASM is often integrated with other security practices such as vulnerability management and threat intelligence.
  • Effective ASM can significantly reduce the risk of data breaches and improve compliance with regulatory requirements.

Cybersecurity & Infrastructure Security Agency

A U.S. government agency responsible for protecting the nation's critical infrastructure from cyber threats.

Description

The Cybersecurity & Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) is a key component of the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) in the United States. Established in 2018, CISA's primary mission is to enhance the security, resilience, and reliability of the nation’s cybersecurity and critical infrastructure. This agency plays a vital role in coordinating the federal government's efforts to protect against and respond to cyber threats and attacks. CISA works closely with state and local governments, private sector organizations, and international partners to share information and best practices related to cybersecurity. Their initiatives include providing cybersecurity assessments, sharing threat intelligence, and offering training programs to enhance the skills of cybersecurity professionals. CISA also leads national efforts to improve the security and resilience of the nation's critical infrastructure sectors, such as energy, transportation, and communications, against both physical and cyber threats. By fostering collaboration and providing resources, CISA aims to create a more secure environment for the American public and critical systems.

Examples

  • CISA provides the Cyber Hygiene program to help organizations assess and improve their cyber defenses.
  • CISA partners with private sector companies to enhance the cybersecurity of critical infrastructure sectors.

Additional Information

  • CISA plays a crucial role in responding to significant cyber incidents and coordinating recovery efforts.
  • The agency offers a range of tools and services, including the Automated Indicator Sharing (AIS) platform for real-time threat information sharing.

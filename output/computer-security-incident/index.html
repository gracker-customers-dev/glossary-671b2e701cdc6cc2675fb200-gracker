computer security incident

An event that compromises the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of computer systems or data.

Description

A computer security incident refers to any event that poses a threat to the security of an organization's computer systems or data. This can include a wide range of occurrences, such as unauthorized access to sensitive data, malware infections, denial of service attacks, data breaches, and other malicious activities that can disrupt normal operations. Incidents may be the result of external attacks, such as hacking or phishing, or internal failures, such as accidental data deletion or misconfigured systems. The identification and management of security incidents are critical components of an organization's security posture, as timely responses can mitigate damage and prevent further exploitation. Organizations typically implement incident response plans to manage the lifecycle of an incident, which includes detection, analysis, containment, eradication, recovery, and lessons learned. Effective incident management helps ensure that systems remain secure, data integrity is maintained, and trust is preserved with stakeholders.

Examples

  • Unauthorized access to a company's database by an external hacker.
  • A ransomware attack that encrypts critical files and demands payment for decryption.

Additional Information

  • Organizations often use security information and event management (SIEM) tools to detect and respond to incidents.
  • Regular training and awareness programs can help employees recognize and report potential security incidents.

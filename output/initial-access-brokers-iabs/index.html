Initial Access Brokers (IABs)

IABs are intermediaries who sell access to compromised networks to other cybercriminals.

Description

Initial Access Brokers (IABs) are a crucial element in the cybercrime ecosystem, acting as intermediaries that facilitate access to compromised networks. These brokers typically gain unauthorized access to organizations' networks through various means, such as phishing, exploiting vulnerabilities, or using stolen credentials. Once they have gained access, they sell this access to other cybercriminals or groups, often for a fee. This practice allows IABs to monetize their skills and resources without having to engage in the more complex stages of cyberattacks, such as data exfiltration or ransomware deployment. IABs often operate within underground forums or dark web marketplaces, where they can advertise their services and connect with potential buyers. The rise of IABs has contributed to the increasing sophistication of cybercrime, as it allows less skilled attackers to leverage the access obtained by IABs to launch their own attacks. This model not only increases the profitability for IABs but also broadens the threat landscape for organizations, as they face potential attacks from multiple fronts.

Examples

  • A broker sells access to a corporate network after breaching its defenses through phishing.
  • An IAB offers access to a collection of ransomware-infected machines for a fee.

Additional Information

  • IABs often use various tools and techniques to maintain stealth and avoid detection.
  • The activities of IABs highlight the importance of robust cybersecurity measures for organizations.

information system resilience

The ability of an information system to anticipate, withstand, recover from, and adapt to adverse conditions, threats, or attacks.

Description

Information system resilience refers to the capacity of an information system to maintain its core functions and recover quickly from disruptions, whether they stem from cyberattacks, natural disasters, or technical failures. In the context of network security, resilience involves implementing strategies and technologies that ensure continuous operation and data protection despite potential threats. This includes designing systems with redundancy, employing robust incident response plans, and regularly updating security measures to mitigate vulnerabilities. Resilient systems are not only focused on preventing attacks but also on minimizing their impact and facilitating a swift recovery. This holistic approach encompasses proactive measures such as threat detection and response, as well as reactive measures like backup systems and disaster recovery protocols. Ultimately, information system resilience is about ensuring that organizations can sustain their operations and protect their information assets, thereby maintaining trust among stakeholders and continuity of service.

Examples

  • Implementation of redundant network paths to ensure connectivity during outages.
  • Regularly scheduled backups and disaster recovery drills to ensure data integrity and availability.

Additional Information

  • Resilience often involves the use of advanced technologies such as AI and machine learning for threat detection.
  • Collaboration among IT, security, and business units is essential for developing a comprehensive resilience strategy.

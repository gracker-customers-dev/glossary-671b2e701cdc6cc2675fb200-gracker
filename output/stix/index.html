STIX

Structured Threat Information Expression

Description

STIX (Structured Threat Information Expression) is a standardized language designed for sharing cyber threat intelligence. It provides a framework for representing and sharing information about threats, vulnerabilities, incidents, and other relevant security-related data in a structured and machine-readable format. STIX allows organizations to convey detailed threat information, including tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) used by adversaries, as well as indicators of compromise (IOCs) such as file hashes, IP addresses, and URLs. The primary goal of STIX is to enable better communication and collaboration among different stakeholders in the cybersecurity community, facilitating the timely sharing of threat intelligence to improve detection, prevention, and response to cyber threats. STIX is often used in conjunction with TAXII (Trusted Automated eXchange of Indicator Information), which provides a transport mechanism for sharing STIX data. By adopting STIX, organizations can enhance their situational awareness and improve their overall security posture through better-informed decision-making and proactive threat hunting.

Examples

  • Sharing threat intelligence reports between organizations using STIX format.
  • Automated threat detection systems that ingest STIX data to identify potential threats.

Additional Information

  • STIX is maintained by the OASIS Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) Technical Committee.
  • There are various tools and libraries available to help organizations implement STIX in their threat intelligence processes.

ict supply chain threat

A risk to information and communication technology systems arising from vulnerabilities in the supply chain.

Description

An ICT supply chain threat refers to potential security risks that can affect information and communication technology systems through vulnerabilities within the supply chain. In today's interconnected world, organizations rely heavily on third-party vendors, hardware manufacturers, and software developers for their technology needs. This reliance introduces vulnerabilities, as malicious actors can exploit weaknesses in the supply chain to insert compromised components or software into legitimate systems. These threats can manifest in various forms, including tampered hardware, malicious software updates, or insecure configurations from third-party suppliers. The consequences of such threats can be severe, leading to data breaches, loss of sensitive information, and disruption of services. Organizations must adopt robust risk management strategies, including thorough vetting of suppliers and continuous monitoring of supply chain activities, to mitigate these risks and ensure the integrity and security of their ICT systems.

Examples

  • Malware embedded in hardware components during manufacturing.
  • A software update from a third-party vendor that contains a backdoor.

Additional Information

  • Supply chain attacks can be difficult to detect and may go unnoticed for extended periods.
  • Organizations should implement a zero-trust security model to safeguard against supply chain threats.

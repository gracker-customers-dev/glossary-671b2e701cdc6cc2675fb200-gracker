Phone phishing

A social engineering attack where attackers use phone calls to deceive individuals into providing sensitive information.

Description

Phone phishing, also known as vishing (voice phishing), is a type of social engineering attack that involves deceiving individuals over the phone into divulging confidential information such as personal identification numbers, credit card details, or account passwords. Attackers often impersonate legitimate entities, such as banks, government agencies, or tech support, to gain the trust of their targets. The calls may involve urgent threats or tempting offers to create a sense of urgency that prompts the victim to comply without thinking critically about the situation. Some attackers use caller ID spoofing techniques to make it appear as though they are calling from a trusted source. As technology evolves, so do the tactics used in phone phishing, including the use of automated systems to reach a larger number of potential victims. Awareness and skepticism are crucial in defending against such attacks, as individuals are often the last line of defense against data breaches and identity theft. Training and education on recognizing phishing attempts are essential for personal and organizational security.

Examples

  • An attacker calls pretending to be from a bank, claiming there's an issue with the account and requests verification of login credentials.
  • A fraudster contacts a victim, posing as a technical support representative from a well-known software company, and asks for remote access to troubleshoot a 'problem'.

Additional Information

  • Victims of phone phishing may suffer financial loss, identity theft, or compromised personal data.
  • It is essential to verify the identity of callers and never provide sensitive information over the phone unless certain of the recipient's legitimacy.

Command & control

The mechanisms and systems used by attackers to maintain communication and control over compromised systems.

Description

In the realm of network security, 'Command & Control' (C&C or C2) refers to the methods that attackers utilize to communicate with and control compromised systems, often referred to as 'bots' or 'zombies'. This communication allows the attacker to send commands, receive data, and manage the infected devices remotely. C&C infrastructure can take various forms, including centralized server architectures, peer-to-peer networks, or even cloud services. Attackers often use various techniques to mask their C&C channels, such as encryption, domain generation algorithms (DGAs), or using legitimate services to obfuscate their activities. This enables them to evade detection by security solutions. C&C is a crucial component of many cyber attack strategies, including Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks, data exfiltration, ransomware deployment, and more. Understanding and disrupting C&C communications is essential for effective incident response and mitigation of ongoing attacks.

Examples

  • A botnet that receives commands from a central server to launch DDoS attacks on specified targets.
  • Malware that connects to a remote server to exfiltrate sensitive data from an infected organization.

Additional Information

  • C&C infrastructure is often designed to be resilient, making it difficult to take down by law enforcement.
  • Security measures include network traffic analysis and monitoring for unusual outbound connections.

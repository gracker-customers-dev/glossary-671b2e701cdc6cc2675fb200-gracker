Whaling

A type of phishing attack targeting high-profile individuals within an organization.

Description

Whaling is a specific form of phishing that focuses on high-level executives or important figures within an organization, such as CEOs, CFOs, or other decision-makers. Unlike standard phishing attacks that target a broad audience, whaling is more sophisticated and often involves detailed research on the target to make the attack more convincing. Attackers may impersonate legitimate business partners, vendors, or even trusted colleagues to trick the target into divulging sensitive information, such as login credentials, financial data, or other confidential information. The goal is to exploit the authority of the individual to gain unauthorized access to corporate resources or to initiate fraudulent transactions. Whaling attacks can take many forms, including emails, phone calls, or fake websites, and they often utilize social engineering tactics to establish trust. Given the potential damage to an organization, including financial loss and reputational harm, whaling requires vigilant security measures and employee training to recognize and respond to such threats.

Examples

  • An attacker sends an email that appears to be from the CEO asking the finance department to transfer funds urgently.
  • A fake invoice is sent to a high-ranking official that looks legitimate, prompting payment to a fraudulent account.

Additional Information

  • Whaling attacks often leverage information gained from social media and public records.
  • Organizations can mitigate whaling risks through awareness training and implementing multi-factor authentication.

Attack Surface Management

The practice of identifying, analyzing, and reducing the potential entry points (or attack surfaces) that malicious actors can exploit in a network.

Description

Attack Surface Management (ASM) is a proactive cybersecurity approach aimed at identifying and mitigating vulnerabilities across an organization's digital landscape. The 'attack surface' refers to all the points in a system where an unauthorized user can try to enter data or extract data from an environment. This includes all hardware, software, network connections, and user interfaces. ASM involves continuous monitoring and assessment of these points to understand the full scope of the attack surface, which can evolve due to changes in technology, infrastructure, and business processes. By employing ASM, organizations can prioritize their security efforts on the most critical vulnerabilities and ensure that they are equipped to defend against the latest threats. ASM tools and methodologies provide insights into the number and types of assets exposed to the internet, allowing security teams to take informed actions, such as patching vulnerabilities, restricting access, and implementing defensive measures to lower risk levels.

Examples

  • Using automated tools to scan for exposed assets and vulnerabilities in real-time.
  • Conducting regular penetration testing to identify weaknesses that may not be visible through conventional scanning.

Additional Information

  • ASM is a critical component of a comprehensive risk management strategy.
  • Effective ASM requires collaboration between IT, security teams, and business units to ensure all assets are accounted for.

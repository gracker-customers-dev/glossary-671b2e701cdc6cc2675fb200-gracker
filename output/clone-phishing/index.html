Clone phishing

A type of phishing attack where a legitimate email is replicated to deceive the recipient.

Description

Clone phishing is a sophisticated form of phishing that involves creating an exact replica of a previously delivered legitimate email. In this attack, cybercriminals take advantage of an existing email communication, often one that the target has already received and trusted. They then modify the original email by replacing a legitimate link or attachment with a malicious one, while keeping the rest of the content identical. The goal is to trick the recipient into believing the email is genuine and prompting them to click on the malicious link or download the infected attachment. Clone phishing exploits the inherent trust the recipient has in the original email and can be particularly effective because it appears to come from a known source. This type of phishing often targets sensitive information or entices the recipient to perform actions that lead to malware installation or data breaches. Awareness and vigilance are crucial defenses against clone phishing attacks, as users must be cautious about unexpected prompts, even from trusted sources.

Examples

  • An attacker clones an email from a bank that the victim has previously received, changing a link to redirect to a phishing site.
  • A corporate employee receives a replicated email from their IT department, which contains a malicious file disguised as a software update.

Additional Information

  • Clone phishing relies heavily on social engineering techniques to exploit user trust.
  • Users should verify unexpected requests for sensitive information, even if the email appears legitimate.

PUP

Potentially Unwanted Program

Description

A Potentially Unwanted Program (PUP) refers to software that may not be malicious but can negatively impact a user's experience. PUPs are often bundled with legitimate software and installed without the user's full knowledge or consent. They can include adware, toolbars, and other applications that may change browser settings, display unwanted advertisements, or collect user data without adequate disclosure. While PUPs are not classified as viruses or malware, they can lead to reduced system performance, unwanted behavior, and privacy concerns. Many antivirus programs and security solutions now include features to detect and remove PUPs, as they can be an annoyance to users and lead to potential security risks if they collect sensitive information. Users are advised to carefully read installation prompts and opt out of additional software to minimize the risk of unintentionally installing PUPs.

Examples

  • Adware that displays unwanted advertisements.
  • Browser toolbars that change the homepage or search engine.

Additional Information

  • PUPs can be included in software bundles, often leading to unintentional installation.
  • Regularly reviewing installed programs can help users identify and remove PUPs.

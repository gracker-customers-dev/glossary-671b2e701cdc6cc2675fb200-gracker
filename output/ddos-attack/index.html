DDoS attack

A Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt the normal functioning of a targeted server, service, or network by overwhelming it with a flood of internet traffic.

Description

A Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attack involves multiple compromised computer systems targeting a single system, often leading to the latter's inability to handle legitimate requests. In a DDoS attack, attackers use a network of infected devices, known as a botnet, to generate a massive volume of traffic directed toward the target. This influx of traffic can exhaust the target's resources, such as bandwidth, processing power, and memory, causing it to slow down significantly or become completely unresponsive. DDoS attacks can take various forms, including volumetric attacks, protocol attacks, and application layer attacks. They can result in significant downtime, financial loss, and damage to an organization’s reputation. As DDoS attacks become more sophisticated, organizations need to implement robust security measures, including traffic analysis, rate limiting, and utilizing DDoS protection services to mitigate these threats effectively.

Examples

  • The Mirai botnet attack, which leveraged IoT devices to launch a massive DDoS attack on DNS provider Dyn in 2016.
  • The 2018 attack on GitHub, which peaked at 1.35 Tbps and was one of the largest recorded DDoS attacks.

Additional Information

  • DDoS attacks can be difficult to prevent and mitigate due to their distributed nature.
  • Many organizations employ DDoS mitigation services that absorb and filter malicious traffic before it reaches the target.

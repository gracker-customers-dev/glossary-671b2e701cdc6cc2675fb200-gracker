TTP

TTP stands for Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures.

Description

In the context of network security, TTP refers to the behaviors and methodologies that attackers use to achieve their objectives. 'Tactics' represent the overall strategy or goal of the attacker, such as gaining unauthorized access or exfiltrating data. 'Techniques' are the general methods employed to achieve these goals, like phishing, exploitation of vulnerabilities, or lateral movement within a network. 'Procedures' are the specific implementations of these techniques, detailing how the attacker carries out their actions in a structured manner. Understanding TTPs is crucial for cybersecurity professionals as it helps in threat intelligence, incident response, and developing defenses against sophisticated cyber threats. By analyzing the TTPs used by threat actors, organizations can better anticipate attacks and develop strategies to mitigate potential risks. This understanding also aids in creating effective detection and response mechanisms to counteract the tactics and techniques employed by cyber adversaries.

Examples

  • An attacker using phishing emails to gain initial access (Technique) as part of their goal to steal sensitive information (Tactic).
  • Utilizing a specific malware strain designed for data exfiltration (Procedure) to achieve the objective of data theft (Tactic).

Additional Information

  • TTPs are often documented in threat intelligence reports to aid organizations in understanding and defending against specific threats.
  • The MITRE ATT&CK framework is a widely used resource that categorizes TTPs based on different adversary behaviors and attack stages.

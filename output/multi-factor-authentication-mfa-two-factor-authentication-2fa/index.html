Multi-factor Authentication (MFA) / Two-factor Authentication (2FA)

A security mechanism that requires two or more verification factors to gain access to a resource.

Description

Multi-factor Authentication (MFA) and Two-factor Authentication (2FA) are security measures that enhance the protection of user accounts and sensitive information by requiring multiple forms of verification before access is granted. MFA involves the use of two or more different factors from the following categories: something you know (like a password), something you have (like a smartphone or hardware token), and something you are (like a fingerprint or facial recognition). 2FA is a subset of MFA that specifically requires two factors. This additional layer of security helps to mitigate risks associated with compromised passwords, as attackers would need to obtain the second form of verification to gain unauthorized access. By implementing MFA/2FA, organizations can significantly reduce the likelihood of data breaches and unauthorized access to systems, making it a critical component of modern cybersecurity strategies.

Examples

  • Using a password in combination with a verification code sent to a mobile device.
  • Logging in with a fingerprint scan after entering a password.

Additional Information

  • MFA can enhance compliance with regulations that require stronger security measures.
  • Many popular services, such as Google and Microsoft, offer MFA options to their users.

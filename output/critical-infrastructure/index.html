critical infrastructure

Assets and systems crucial for the functioning of society and economy.

Description

Critical infrastructure refers to the essential systems and assets that are necessary for the functioning of a society and its economy. These infrastructures include sectors such as energy, transportation, water supply, telecommunications, and emergency services. In the context of network security, critical infrastructure is particularly important because these systems are often targets for cyberattacks that can disrupt services, cause economic loss, or threaten public safety. Protecting critical infrastructure involves implementing robust security measures to safeguard against both physical and cyber threats. This includes risk assessments, incident response plans, and resilience strategies to ensure that essential services remain operational, even in the face of disruptions. The interdependencies among various critical infrastructure sectors mean that a failure in one area can have cascading effects on others, highlighting the importance of comprehensive security approaches and collaboration among stakeholders at all levels.

Examples

  • Electric power generation and distribution systems
  • Water treatment and distribution facilities

Additional Information

  • Cybersecurity frameworks like NIST are often applied to protect critical infrastructure.
  • Public-private partnerships are crucial for securing critical infrastructure against evolving threats.

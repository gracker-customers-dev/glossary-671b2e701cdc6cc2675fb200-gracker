macro virus

A type of malware that is embedded in macro-enabled documents.

Description

A macro virus is a type of computer virus that is written in the macro programming language of applications, such as Microsoft Word or Excel. These viruses are typically spread through infected documents that contain malicious macros. When a user opens such a document and enables macros, the virus is executed, potentially leading to unauthorized actions on the user’s system. Macro viruses can replicate themselves and infect other documents, making them a particularly insidious form of malware. They can cause various types of damage, including corrupting files, stealing sensitive information, and spreading to other computers via shared documents. The use of macro viruses became widespread in the 1990s, when many users were unaware of the risks associated with enabling macros. Effective protection against macro viruses includes using updated antivirus software, educating users about the risks of enabling macros from unknown sources, and employing security measures such as disabling macros by default in applications.

Examples

  • Concept virus - a macro virus that spreads through Microsoft Word documents, often changing the content of the document.
  • Melissa virus - one of the first macro viruses that spread via email attachments, causing significant disruptions in the late 1990s.

Additional Information

  • Macro viruses often exploit the trust users have in familiar applications.
  • Best practices include keeping software up to date and avoiding opening documents from unknown sources.

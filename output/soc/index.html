SOC

Security Operations Center

Description

A Security Operations Center (SOC) is a centralized unit that deals with security issues on an organizational and technical level. The primary purpose of a SOC is to monitor, detect, respond to, and mitigate cybersecurity threats in real-time. The SOC is staffed with security analysts and engineers who utilize a variety of tools and technologies to protect the organization’s information and infrastructure from cyber attacks. They analyze security alerts generated by applications and network hardware, investigate breaches, and respond to incidents. The SOC operates 24/7 to ensure continuous vigilance against evolving threats. By employing threat intelligence and incident response protocols, the SOC plays a critical role in maintaining the security posture of an organization. Furthermore, it serves as a hub for compliance and governance, ensuring that security policies and regulatory requirements are adhered to. In today’s digital landscape, a well-functioning SOC is essential for proactive defense against cyber threats and for minimizing the impact of security incidents.

Examples

  • A SOC team detects and responds to a ransomware attack on an organization's network.
  • The SOC analyzes suspicious user behavior to identify potential insider threats.

Additional Information

  • SOC can be in-house, outsourced, or a combination of both (hybrid model).
  • Key technologies used in a SOC include SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) systems, intrusion detection/prevention systems, and threat intelligence platforms.

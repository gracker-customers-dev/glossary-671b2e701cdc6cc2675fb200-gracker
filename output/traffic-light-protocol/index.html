traffic light protocol

A standardized method for sharing sensitive information about security incidents and vulnerabilities among organizations.

Description

The Traffic Light Protocol (TLP) is a system for sharing sensitive information in a way that indicates the handling and dissemination restrictions associated with that information. It is commonly used in the context of cybersecurity, where timely and secure communication about threats is crucial. TLP categorizes information into four color-coded levels: TLP:RED (restricted to individuals within a specific organization), TLP:AMBER (limited distribution within a community), TLP:GREEN (shared with the broader community but not for public release), and TLP:WHITE (unrestricted and can be shared publicly). This protocol helps organizations to manage the flow of information securely, ensuring that sensitive data is not improperly disclosed while still allowing for effective collaboration and threat intelligence sharing. By using TLP, organizations can quickly convey the sensitivity of the information, thus enabling recipients to act appropriately based on the shared content's classification.

Examples

  • A company receives a TLP:RED alert regarding a zero-day vulnerability affecting their systems.
  • An industry group shares a TLP:GREEN report about a new phishing campaign targeting their sector.

Additional Information

  • TLP was developed by the Forum of Incident Response and Security Teams (FIRST).
  • It is widely adopted by government and private sector organizations for incident reporting and information sharing.

CIS

CIS stands for the Center for Internet Security, a nonprofit organization dedicated to enhancing the cybersecurity posture of organizations.

Description

The Center for Internet Security (CIS) is a nonprofit organization that focuses on improving the cybersecurity of public and private sector organizations. CIS is well-known for its development of the CIS Controls, a set of best practices designed to help organizations improve their defenses against cybersecurity threats. These controls are prioritized actions that can help mitigate the most common attack vectors. Additionally, CIS provides the CIS Benchmarks, which are security configuration guidelines for various technologies, including operating systems, cloud environments, and applications. These benchmarks serve as a standard for organizations to enhance their security measures. By adopting CIS benchmarks and controls, organizations can establish a baseline security posture and improve their resilience against cyber threats. The CIS also facilitates information sharing through its community of cybersecurity professionals, helping organizations stay informed about the latest threats and vulnerabilities. Overall, CIS plays a crucial role in shaping cybersecurity standards and practices globally.

Examples

  • CIS Controls: A set of 20 actions to improve cybersecurity.
  • CIS Benchmarks: Configuration guidelines for securing systems.

Additional Information

  • CIS collaborates with various organizations to create and maintain its resources.
  • Membership to CIS is open to individuals and organizations seeking to enhance their cybersecurity initiatives.

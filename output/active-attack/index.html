active attack

An attack where an unauthorized entity attempts to alter system resources or affect their operation.

Description

In network security, an active attack refers to any attempt by an unauthorized user or entity to manipulate system resources or data within a network. Unlike passive attacks, which involve eavesdropping on or monitoring communications, active attacks involve direct interference with the data or processes of a system. This type of attack can manifest in various forms, including but not limited to, data modification, denial of service (DoS), man-in-the-middle attacks, and session hijacking. Active attacks can compromise the integrity, availability, and confidentiality of information systems and networks, leading to significant damage, data loss, or unauthorized access to sensitive information. Organizations must employ robust security measures, such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and encryption, to mitigate the risks associated with active attacks. Continuous monitoring and incident response planning are also essential to detect and respond to such attacks promptly.

Examples

  • Data modification, where an attacker alters data in transit.
  • Denial of Service (DoS) attacks that overwhelm a system to make it unavailable.

Additional Information

  • Active attacks require immediate response and mitigation strategies.
  • They often involve tools and techniques such as malware, packet injection, or session hijacking.

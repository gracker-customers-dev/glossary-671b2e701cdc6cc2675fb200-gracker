British Standard 7799

A standard for information security management that provides a framework for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and improving information security management systems.

Description

British Standard 7799, originally published in 1995, is a standard that outlines best practices for information security management. It was developed to help organizations protect their information assets through effective security management practices. The standard consists of two parts: Part 1 focuses on the specification of an information security management system (ISMS), while Part 2 provides a code of practice for information security management. BS 7799 has been widely adopted by organizations seeking to implement robust security measures and is considered a precursor to the ISO/IEC 27001 standard, which further formalized the requirements for an ISMS. The framework emphasizes risk management and the importance of continuous improvement in security practices. Organizations that comply with BS 7799 can demonstrate their commitment to security and gain a competitive advantage while also improving their overall risk management capabilities.

Examples

  • An organization implementing BS 7799 to enhance its data protection measures.
  • A business seeking certification to BS 7799 to assure clients of its security practices.

Additional Information

  • BS 7799 has been succeeded by ISO/IEC 27001 and ISO/IEC 27002.
  • Organizations often use BS 7799 as a stepping stone towards achieving ISO certification.

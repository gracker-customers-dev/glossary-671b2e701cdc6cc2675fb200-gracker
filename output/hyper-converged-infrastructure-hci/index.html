Hyper converged infrastructure (HCI)

A software-centric architecture that tightly integrates compute, storage, networking, and virtualization resources in a single system.

Description

Hyper-converged infrastructure (HCI) is an IT framework that combines storage, computing, networking, and virtualization into a single, unified system. This architecture simplifies data center management and enhances scalability by allowing organizations to deploy and manage IT resources through a single software platform. HCI typically utilizes commodity hardware, which reduces costs and increases flexibility. From a network security perspective, HCI can bolster security by enabling centralized management of security policies and providing intrinsic security features like encryption and micro-segmentation. HCI can also facilitate disaster recovery and backup solutions more efficiently, ensuring that security measures are maintained across virtualized resources. As organizations increasingly adopt cloud strategies and hybrid environments, HCI offers a robust solution to streamline operations while maintaining a strong security posture in a rapidly changing cyber landscape.

Examples

  • Nutanix: A leader in HCI that offers a platform integrating storage, compute, and virtualization.
  • VMware vSAN: A software-defined storage solution that is part of VMware's HCI offering, allowing for a seamless integration of storage with virtualization.

Additional Information

  • HCI solutions can significantly reduce the footprint of data centers, making them more energy-efficient.
  • Due to their scalability, HCI systems can be tailored to fit the precise needs of different business units, allowing for customized security configurations.

TSA

Time Stamping Authority

Description

A Time Stamping Authority (TSA) is a trusted third party that issues time-stamped tokens, which serve as digital certificates that confirm the existence of a particular piece of data at a specific time. TSAs are essential in various applications where the integrity and authenticity of data are crucial, such as in legal documents, digital signatures, and electronic commerce. When a TSA provides a time stamp, it attaches a cryptographic signature to the time-stamped data, ensuring that the timestamp cannot be altered without invalidating the signature. This process provides a reliable method for proving when a document was created or modified. The use of TSAs enhances trust in electronic transactions by ensuring that the parties involved can verify that the data existed at a particular point in time, even after the document has been transmitted or stored. TSAs often rely on secure time sources, such as atomic clocks, to ensure the accuracy of the timestamps they provide.

Examples

  • A TSA can provide timestamps for signed contracts to verify their creation date.
  • In software distribution, a TSA can timestamp the code to prove it was created before a certain date.

Additional Information

  • TSAs are often part of Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) systems.
  • The use of TSAs is regulated in various compliance frameworks, such as eIDAS in the European Union.

Lateral Movement

The technique used by attackers to move within a network after initially compromising a system.

Description

Lateral movement refers to the methods that cyber attackers use to spread through a network after gaining access to a single machine. Once an attacker compromises a device, they can explore the network to find sensitive data, escalate privileges, or deploy malicious software. This movement is often stealthy, leveraging legitimate tools and processes to avoid detection by security systems. Attackers may use various techniques such as exploiting vulnerabilities, utilizing stolen credentials, or taking advantage of misconfigured systems to traverse the network. Effective lateral movement can significantly increase the impact of a breach, as it allows attackers to target critical infrastructure, sensitive information, or other high-value assets. Organizations can mitigate lateral movement risks by implementing strict access controls, segmenting networks, monitoring for unusual activity, and regularly auditing their IT environments to identify and close security gaps.

Examples

  • An attacker uses stolen credentials to access additional servers within a network.
  • An attacker exploits a vulnerability in a workstation to gain access to the domain controller.

Additional Information

  • Lateral movement is often part of a larger attack, such as ransomware deployment or data exfiltration.
  • Tools like Mimikatz and PowerShell are commonly used for lateral movement activities.

APT10

APT10 is a cyber espionage group believed to be associated with the Chinese government.

Description

APT10, also known as Stone Panda, is a sophisticated advanced persistent threat (APT) group that has been active since at least 2009. This group is known for conducting cyber espionage campaigns targeting a wide range of industries, including technology, telecommunications, and healthcare. APT10 is particularly noted for its use of cloud computing services to facilitate its attacks and for its focus on stealing sensitive information from foreign organizations. The group employs a variety of tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) to exploit vulnerabilities and gain unauthorized access to systems. Their operations often involve spear-phishing emails, credential harvesting, and leveraging remotely accessible services to maintain persistence within victim networks. APT10's activities have drawn attention from cybersecurity agencies globally, leading to increased awareness and defensive measures against such threats. The group's ability to adapt and evolve its methods makes it a persistent threat to national and corporate security.

Examples

  • APT10 was involved in the attack on the U.S. government and private sector organizations, stealing sensitive data.
  • The group has targeted managed service providers (MSPs) to gain access to their clients' networks.

Additional Information

  • APT10 is linked to the Chinese Ministry of State Security.
  • The group has been known to exploit cloud services to enhance its operations.

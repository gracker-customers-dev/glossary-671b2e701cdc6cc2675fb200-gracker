Cryptojacking

Cryptojacking is the unauthorized use of someone else's computer to mine cryptocurrency.

Description

Cryptojacking is a form of cyber attack where an attacker covertly uses a victim's computing resources to mine cryptocurrency without their consent. This typically occurs through malicious scripts embedded in websites or through infected software that runs in the background of the victim's device. The impact of cryptojacking can be significant, as it can slow down the victim's device, increase electricity costs, and even lead to hardware damage due to excessive resource consumption. Cryptojackers often target devices with high processing power, such as servers, desktops, and even IoT devices. Unlike traditional malware, which often aims to steal sensitive information, cryptojacking focuses on using the victim's resources for profit. Detection can be challenging, as the symptoms may not be immediately apparent. Organizations are encouraged to implement security measures such as regular system updates, using ad blockers, and monitoring system performance to mitigate the risks associated with cryptojacking.

Examples

  • A website that runs a JavaScript miner to utilize visitors' CPU power without their knowledge.
  • An employee unknowingly downloading an infected application that uses their work computer to mine cryptocurrency.

Additional Information

  • Cryptojacking can be more profitable for attackers compared to ransomware since it does not require the attacker to interact with the victim after the initial infection.
  • Many security solutions now include detection capabilities for cryptojacking scripts and activities.

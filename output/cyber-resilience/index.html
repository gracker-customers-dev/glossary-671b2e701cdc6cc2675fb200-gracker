Cyber resilience

The ability of an organization to prepare for, respond to, and recover from cyber incidents.

Description

Cyber resilience refers to an organization's capability to withstand, adapt to, and recover from cyber threats and incidents. It encompasses a holistic approach that combines preventive measures, detection capabilities, and responsive strategies to ensure that critical operations can continue or quickly resume after a disruption. Cyber resilience goes beyond traditional cybersecurity, which often focuses predominantly on preventing breaches, by acknowledging that breaches may occur despite best efforts. Therefore, it emphasizes the importance of planning for potential disruptions and developing robust incident response and recovery plans. Key components of cyber resilience include risk management, continuous monitoring of systems, employee training and awareness, and the implementation of backup solutions. Organizations that adopt a cyber resilience framework are better equipped to handle the complexities of the modern cyber threat landscape, ensuring both operational continuity and the protection of sensitive information.

Examples

  • Implementing a comprehensive incident response plan that includes regular drills and updates.
  • Utilizing multi-layered security approaches, such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and encryption.

Additional Information

  • Cyber resilience requires collaboration across all levels of an organization, from executive management to IT staff.
  • It is increasingly being recognized as a critical component of overall business resilience strategies.

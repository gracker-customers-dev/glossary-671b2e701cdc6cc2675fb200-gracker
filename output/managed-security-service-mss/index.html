Managed security service (MSS)

A service that provides outsourced monitoring and management of security devices and systems.

Description

Managed Security Services (MSS) refers to the practice of outsourcing network security functions to a third-party service provider. MSS providers typically offer a range of security services including continuous monitoring, incident response, threat intelligence, vulnerability management, and compliance reporting. Businesses leverage MSS to enhance their security posture without the overhead of maintaining extensive in-house security teams and infrastructure. The MSS provider utilizes advanced tools and technologies to detect, analyze, and respond to security threats in real-time. This allows organizations to benefit from expert knowledge and resources that may not be available internally, enabling them to focus on their core business functions while ensuring their networks and data are protected from emerging threats. Furthermore, MSS can provide access to 24/7 security operations centers (SOCs), ensuring constant vigilance against potential security incidents.

Examples

  • 24/7 network monitoring and incident response services
  • Vulnerability assessments and penetration testing

Additional Information

  • MSS can help organizations comply with industry regulations such as GDPR or HIPAA.
  • MSS providers often use a combination of automated tools and human expertise to deliver comprehensive security.

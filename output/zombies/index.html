Zombies

Zombies are compromised computers that are controlled by an attacker to perform malicious activities without the owner's knowledge.

Description

In the context of network security, 'zombies' refer to computers that have been infected with malware, allowing cybercriminals to remotely control them. These infected machines can be used as part of a botnet, a network of such compromised devices, to carry out a variety of attacks, such as sending spam emails, launching Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks, or stealing sensitive information. The owner of a zombie computer is usually unaware that their system has been infected, making it a stealthy and effective tool for attackers. Zombies can be created through various means, including phishing attacks, malicious downloads, or exploiting software vulnerabilities. The management and mitigation of zombie networks are critical for maintaining cybersecurity, as they pose significant risks to both individuals and organizations. Regular updates, antivirus scans, and user education are essential to prevent systems from becoming zombies and being exploited by malicious actors.

Examples

  • A computer infected with a Trojan horse that sends spam emails without the user's knowledge.
  • A smart device hijacked to participate in a DDoS attack against a targeted website.

Additional Information

  • Zombies are often part of larger botnets, which can consist of thousands or even millions of infected devices.
  • Detecting and removing zombies requires specialized security tools and practices, including network monitoring and behavioral analysis.

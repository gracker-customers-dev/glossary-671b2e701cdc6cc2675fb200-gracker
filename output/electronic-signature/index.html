electronic signature

A digital representation of a person's intent to agree to the contents of a document or transaction.

Description

An electronic signature, often referred to as e-signature, is a digital form of a signature that is used to indicate a person's consent or approval of the contents of a document or transaction. In the context of network security, electronic signatures are crucial for ensuring the authenticity and integrity of electronic communications. They utilize cryptographic techniques to bind the signer's identity to the signed document, ensuring that any alterations to the document after signing can be detected. Electronic signatures can take various forms, including typed names, scanned images of handwritten signatures, or more secure digital signatures that rely on public key infrastructure (PKI). With the rise of electronic transactions, e-signatures have gained legal recognition under laws like the Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce (ESIGN) Act in the U.S. and the eIDAS regulation in the EU, making them a valid alternative to traditional handwritten signatures. The use of e-signatures enhances efficiency, reduces paper usage, and speeds up the transaction process while maintaining a high level of security.

Examples

  • A scanned image of a handwritten signature included in a PDF document.
  • A digital signature generated using a private key to sign an electronic contract.

Additional Information

  • Electronic signatures are legally binding in many jurisdictions, provided they meet certain criteria.
  • They can be used in various applications, including contracts, agreements, and forms in both business and personal transactions.

known bad

A term used to describe entities, such as IP addresses, files, or URLs, that are recognized as malicious or harmful based on prior evidence or threat intelligence.

Description

In network security, 'known bad' refers to any digital entity that has been identified through historical data or threat intelligence as harmful or malicious. This identification often comes from previous attacks, malware analysis, or documented incidents that have been cataloged by security experts. The term is commonly associated with blacklists that security systems use to filter out known threats. For example, an IP address may be flagged as 'known bad' if it has been involved in cyberattacks or associated with malicious communications in the past. Security solutions leverage this knowledge to enhance protective measures, automatically blocking or alerting users about these 'known bad' entities. This approach allows organizations to proactively defend their networks against threats without waiting for new attacks to occur. The use of 'known bad' data is crucial in threat detection and incident response, forming a foundational component of many cybersecurity strategies.

Examples

  • An IP address that has been reported for sending spam or participating in a DDoS attack.
  • A URL that hosts malware and has been classified by security vendors as dangerous.

Additional Information

  • Known bad entities are often used in conjunction with threat intelligence feeds to keep security measures up-to-date.
  • The effectiveness of using known bad lists is limited by the dynamic nature of threats; new threats can emerge that are not yet documented.

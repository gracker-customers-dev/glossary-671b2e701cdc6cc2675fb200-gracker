VSM

VSM stands for Vulnerability Scanning and Management.

Description

Vulnerability Scanning and Management (VSM) is a critical process in network security that involves identifying, classifying, and mitigating vulnerabilities in systems and applications. It is an ongoing practice that is vital for maintaining the security posture of an organization. VSM typically starts with automated vulnerability scans that detect known vulnerabilities within the network, operating systems, and applications. These scans utilize databases such as the Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVE) list to identify vulnerabilities that may be exploited by attackers. After scanning, the identified vulnerabilities are prioritized based on their severity, potential impact, and the likelihood of exploitation. Organizations then implement remediation strategies, which may include patching software, reconfiguring systems, or applying other security measures to mitigate risks. VSM not only helps in compliance with various regulations and standards but also enhances the overall security framework of the organization by proactively addressing potential security weaknesses before they can be exploited.

Examples

  • Using Nessus to perform vulnerability scans on network devices.
  • Implementing a continuous vulnerability management strategy in line with NIST guidelines.

Additional Information

  • VSM is essential for compliance with standards like PCI DSS and ISO 27001.
  • Regular vulnerability assessments can help in early detection of security threats.

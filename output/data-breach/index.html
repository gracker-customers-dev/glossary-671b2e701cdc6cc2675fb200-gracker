data breach

An unauthorized access and retrieval of sensitive information from a system.

Description

A data breach occurs when an individual or group gains unauthorized access to confidential data, typically held by organizations or individuals. This can involve sensitive personal information such as social security numbers, credit card details, health records, and proprietary business data. Data breaches can happen due to various reasons, including hacking, accidental data exposure, insider threats, or physical theft of devices. The consequences of a data breach can be severe, leading to financial loss, reputational damage, and legal implications for organizations. Organizations are often required to notify affected individuals and take steps to mitigate the damage, which may include offering credit monitoring services and enhancing security measures. The increasing frequency and sophistication of data breaches highlight the importance of robust cybersecurity practices and data protection regulations to safeguard sensitive information from unauthorized access.

Examples

  • The Equifax data breach in 2017 exposed the personal information of approximately 147 million people.
  • The Yahoo data breach in 2013 and 2014 affected all 3 billion user accounts, compromising emails and personal data.

Additional Information

  • Data breaches can result in identity theft and financial fraud.
  • Organizations may face regulatory fines and penalties for failing to protect personal data.

non-repudiation

A security principle that ensures a party cannot deny the authenticity of their signature or the sending of a message.

Description

Non-repudiation is a key concept in network security that ensures that once a transaction, communication, or action has taken place, the involved parties cannot deny their involvement. This principle is crucial for maintaining trust and accountability in digital communications and transactions. Non-repudiation is typically achieved through the use of cryptographic techniques such as digital signatures and secure logging systems. For example, when a user sends a signed email, the digital signature serves as proof that the sender cannot later deny having sent the message. Similarly, when transactions are logged in a secure manner, the records provide verifiable evidence of what actions were taken, by whom, and when. Non-repudiation helps to prevent disputes and fraudulent activities by ensuring that the evidence of actions taken in a network can be referenced and verified. It is an essential component of legal and secure communications, particularly in sectors like finance, e-commerce, and legal documents.

Examples

  • Digital signatures used in email communications to prove authorship.
  • Transaction logs in online banking that record actions taken by users.

Additional Information

  • Non-repudiation is often implemented through public key infrastructure (PKI).
  • It is important for compliance with regulations in various industries.

supply chain

A system of organizations, people, activities, information, and resources involved in supplying products and services to consumers.

Description

In the context of network security, the term 'supply chain' refers to the complex network of entities involved in the development, production, and distribution of hardware and software components. Each link in the supply chain, from raw materials to end-users, can introduce vulnerabilities that could be exploited by malicious actors. Threats can arise from compromised components, whether through intentional tampering or accidental vulnerabilities. As organizations increasingly rely on third-party vendors for software development and hardware manufacturing, the security of the supply chain has become a critical concern. Effective supply chain security involves assessing and mitigating risks at each stage, from design and procurement to deployment and maintenance. This includes ensuring that suppliers adhere to security standards, conducting regular audits, and implementing strategies to detect and respond to security incidents that originate within the supply chain. Organizations must adopt a holistic approach to supply chain security to protect their systems and data from potential breaches stemming from third-party components.

Examples

  • The SolarWinds cyberattack, which exploited vulnerabilities in the software supply chain.
  • Hardware backdoors introduced during the manufacturing process of networking devices.

Additional Information

  • Supply chain attacks can be difficult to detect and often go unnoticed until significant damage has occurred.
  • Organizations are encouraged to implement a Zero Trust model to enhance supply chain security.

Man-In-The-Middle Attack (MitM)

A type of cyber attack where a malicious actor intercepts and relays communications between two parties without their knowledge.

Description

A Man-In-The-Middle (MitM) attack occurs when an attacker secretly intercepts and relays messages between two parties who believe they are communicating directly with each other. This type of attack can happen in various forms, such as through unsecured Wi-Fi networks, where attackers can eavesdrop on data being transmitted. In an MitM scenario, the attacker can capture sensitive information, such as login credentials, personal data, and financial details. The attacker may also alter the communication, injecting malicious content or misleading information. MitM attacks can exploit vulnerabilities in network protocols or use social engineering tactics to gain access to secure communications. To prevent MitM attacks, it is vital to implement security measures such as using encryption (like HTTPS), employing secure protocols, and being vigilant about the security of network connections. Regular software updates and security patches can also help to mitigate the risk of such attacks.

Examples

  • An attacker intercepts a user's connection to a public Wi-Fi network and captures login credentials sent over an unencrypted connection.
  • A malicious actor impersonates a legitimate website, tricking users into entering sensitive information like credit card details.

Additional Information

  • MitM attacks can be conducted through techniques such as ARP spoofing, DNS spoofing, or session hijacking.
  • Using Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) can help secure communications and reduce the risk of MitM attacks.

OASIS TC

OASIS Technical Committee focused on security standards.

Description

OASIS TC, or OASIS Technical Committee, refers to a collaborative group within the Organization for the Advancement of Structured Information Standards (OASIS), which is responsible for developing and maintaining standards in various domains, including network security. The OASIS TC works on creating frameworks, protocols, and specifications that enhance the interoperability and security of information systems. Within the realm of network security, the OASIS TC has developed key standards such as Security Assertion Markup Language (SAML) and eXtensible Access Control Markup Language (XACML), which facilitate secure data exchange and access control among diverse systems. The work of the OASIS TC helps organizations implement robust security measures, ensuring the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of data across networks. By involving a wide range of stakeholders, including industry professionals, academia, and government representatives, the OASIS TC promotes a consensus-driven approach to security standards that address contemporary challenges in cybersecurity.

Examples

  • Development of SAML for secure single sign-on authentication.
  • Creation of XACML for fine-grained access control policies.

Additional Information

  • OASIS TC collaborates with other organizations to ensure broad adoption of standards.
  • Membership in OASIS TC is open to individuals and organizations interested in contributing to the development of security standards.

air gap

A security measure that involves isolating a computer or network from other networks, particularly the internet.

Description

An air gap is a security principle used to protect sensitive information and critical infrastructure by physically isolating systems from unsecured networks, especially the internet. This method ensures that the air-gapped system cannot be accessed remotely, thereby reducing the risk of cyber attacks, malware infections, and unauthorized data access. Organizations that handle highly sensitive data, such as government agencies, military installations, and financial institutions, often employ air gaps to secure their most critical systems. While air gaps provide a strong layer of security, they also introduce challenges in terms of data transfer and system updates, as transferring data often requires physical media, which can become a potential vector for compromise if not managed carefully. Overall, air gaps are an effective defense strategy for systems that require the highest level of security against external threats.

Examples

  • A military facility that operates critical systems completely disconnected from the internet.
  • A financial institution using air-gapped networks to store sensitive customer information.

Additional Information

  • Air gaps can be breached through physical access, social engineering, or insider threats.
  • Modern techniques like data diodes can help maintain air gap integrity while enabling controlled data transfer.

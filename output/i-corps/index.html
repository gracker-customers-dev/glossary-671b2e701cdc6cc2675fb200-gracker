I-Corps

I-Corps refers to a program that helps develop cybersecurity capabilities in organizations, particularly in the context of incident response.

Description

I-Corps, or Incident Corps, is a structured program designed to enhance the cybersecurity posture of organizations by focusing on incident response and management. It provides a framework for organizations to better prepare for, respond to, and recover from cybersecurity incidents. The I-Corps program typically involves training, resources, and collaborative exercises that simulate real-world cyber threats and breaches. By participating in I-Corps, organizations can foster a culture of cybersecurity awareness, improve their incident response capabilities, and develop a robust plan to mitigate the impact of potential attacks. The program emphasizes the importance of a coordinated response involving all stakeholders, including IT, security teams, and executive leadership. This holistic approach ensures that organizations are not only prepared to respond to incidents but can also learn from them to improve their security measures continuously.

Examples

  • Participation in I-Corps training exercises to simulate cyber attack scenarios.
  • Developing an incident response plan as part of I-Corps engagement.

Additional Information

  • I-Corps initiatives often involve collaboration with government agencies and private sector partners.
  • The program may include assessments of current security protocols and recommendations for improvement.

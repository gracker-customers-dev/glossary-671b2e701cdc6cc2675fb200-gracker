Managed intelligence

A proactive approach to cybersecurity that combines advanced analytics, threat intelligence, and human expertise.

Description

Managed intelligence refers to the comprehensive strategy in network security that integrates automated systems with human oversight to enhance the detection and response to threats. It involves the collection and analysis of vast amounts of data from various sources, including network traffic, user behavior, and known threat databases. By employing machine learning and artificial intelligence, managed intelligence solutions can identify anomalies and potential threats in real-time. However, the human element remains crucial, as cybersecurity experts interpret the data, provide context, and make informed decisions on threat mitigation. This dual approach allows organizations to stay ahead of cyber threats, reduce response times, and better allocate resources. Managed intelligence is especially beneficial for organizations lacking in-house cybersecurity expertise, as it provides access to a broader range of skills and knowledge. Overall, the synergy of technology and human analysis creates a robust defense mechanism against evolving cyber threats.

Examples

  • Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) solutions that leverage both automated alerts and analyst insights.
  • Threat intelligence platforms that aggregate data from multiple sources and provide actionable intelligence to security teams.

Additional Information

  • Managed intelligence solutions can significantly reduce the workload on security teams by automating routine tasks.
  • These systems often include continuous monitoring and real-time incident response capabilities.

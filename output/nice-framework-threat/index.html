NICE Framework threat

A threat identified within the NICE Framework that poses a risk to information systems and networks.

Description

The NICE Framework, developed by the National Initiative for Cybersecurity Education, categorizes and defines the various roles and responsibilities in cybersecurity. Within this framework, 'threats' refer to potential events that can exploit vulnerabilities in an organization's information systems, leading to unauthorized access, data breaches, or other harmful consequences. These threats can originate from various sources, including external attackers, insider threats, or natural disasters. The framework helps organizations understand these threats in the context of cybersecurity roles, ensuring that the right skills and knowledge are applied to mitigate them effectively. By recognizing and categorizing threats, organizations can prioritize their cybersecurity efforts, implement appropriate defenses, and develop incident response strategies. This proactive approach is critical in maintaining the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of information assets, which are essential for organizational operations and trust.

Examples

  • Phishing attacks targeting employees to gain access to sensitive data.
  • Ransomware attacks that encrypt organizational data for ransom.

Additional Information

  • The NICE Framework aligns cybersecurity roles with necessary competencies and skills.
  • Threats are categorized into specific domains to streamline training and response efforts.
